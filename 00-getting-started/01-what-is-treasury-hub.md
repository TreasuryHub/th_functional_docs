# What is Treasury Hub?

> **Availability:** `Available` ✅
> **Who this is for:** everyone using the platform.

## Overview
Treasury Hub is a **Treasury Management System (TMS)** that gives your organization a single,
real-time view of cash and financial operations across every bank, currency, and legal entity you
work with. Instead of logging into many bank portals, stitching together spreadsheets, and
reconciling statements by hand, your team works from one platform that ingests data automatically,
reconciles it, and turns it into reporting, payments, and decisions.

It is built for organizations that manage **multiple bank accounts across multiple institutions,
currencies, and companies** — corporate treasuries, finance teams, and financial institutions.

## The problem it solves
Corporate treasury is usually fragmented:
- Accounts spread across dozens of banks, each with its own portal and file format.
- Balances and movements arriving in different formats (MT940, CAMT.053, BAI2, CSV, PDF…).
- Manual, error-prone reconciliation and month-end close.
- No single, trustworthy, real-time cash position.

Treasury Hub consolidates all of this into one place and automates the flow from **raw bank/ERP
data → normalized data → reconciliation → reporting, payments and accounting**.

## How the platform is organized (the 5-layer stack)
Treasury Hub is **AI-native and end-to-end** — data flows from your sources all the way to the
screens you use, and AI assistance is available across every layer.

1. **Sources** — where your data comes from: banks, core banking, PSPs, ERPs, trading venues,
   files, email, and more.
2. **Integrations** — the connectors that bring that data in (SWIFT, Open Banking, APIs, file
   import, email/SFTP ingestion) and send data out.
3. **Data layer** — everything is normalized, unified, and enriched into consistent entities
   (accounts, movements, invoices, deals, loans, investments…).
4. **Workflow layer** — orchestrated, configurable, auditable processes run on top of that data
   (payments, FX, reconciliation, approvals, reporting).
5. **Modules** — what you actually see and use: dashboards, reconciliation, payments, reporting,
   risk, accounting, and administration.

A transversal **AI layer** and a set of **platform services** (integrations, workflows, agents,
admin/UI) are shared across every module — there are no per-module silos.

## What you can do with it
- **See your cash** in real time, grouped by currency, company, country, and bank.
- **Bring in data automatically** from banks (SWIFT / Open Banking), ERPs (NetSuite), files, and
  email/SFTP.
- **Reconcile** bank statements, flows, and ERP data with rule-based and AI-assisted matching.
- **Make payments** with multi-level approvals and full status tracking.
- **Report and forecast** cash and build your own reports (what-if scenario modeling is *in preview*).
- **Manage risk** (in preview): FX, credit, interest-rate, mark-to-market, and ALM.
- **Post to your G/L / ERP** with a rules engine and approval workflow.
- **Administer** users, roles, companies, tags, and access tokens.
